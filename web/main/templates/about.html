BibHelioTech aims at identifying temporal expressions (time intervals) in scientific papers from the Heliophysics domain and linking them to their observational context: satellites, instruments, spatial regions. It connects to Istex, a large database of papers authorized to text and data mining.

BibHelioTech generates standardized catalogues of events which may be visualized in data analysis tools, such as AMDA or Sciqlop.

BibHelioTech was developed at IRAP (Institut de Recherche en Astrophysique et Planétologie, Toulouse ) in the frame of an internship by A. Dablanc supervised by V. Génot.

This web interface is developed by R. Hitier.

Pipeline Hints:
  • First, SUTIME is run to discover time intervals called Durations.
  • Then we search for Missions and Instruments in the text.
  • A correspondance is then done between Missions and Durations
  • That allows us to set some indicators:
    • D: is the distance in the text ( number of characters) between the Duration and the Mission
    • R: is the rank of the mission, meaning how many midssions we had to skip in the text befor making the correspondance with the Duration
    • Conf: is an indice, wich formula is: D × R / (SO × sat_occ) where SO is the total number of missions, and sat_occ the occurences of that specific mission.
    • NConf: is this indice normalized on the whole database
